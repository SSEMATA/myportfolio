Progress on the Project

Phase 1: Research & Planning

We started by defining our objectives, focusing on optimizing traffic flow, ensuring pedestrian safety, and prioritizing emergency vehicles. To establish a solid foundation, we researched existing traffic management systems and identified their limitations. We studied how ultrasonic sensors, Arduino microcontrollers, buzzers, and LED signals function within an automated system. With this knowledge, we designed a system architecture, outlining sensor placements, traffic light control logic, and power distribution.

Phase 2: System Design & Simulation

We translated our research into a practical design by developing a circuit diagram that integrated sensors, lights, and controllers. Using simulation tools like Tinkercad and Proteus, we tested our system logic virtually before moving to hardware. We also created a flowchart outlining the decision-making process for dynamic traffic control. This phase helped us identify potential inefficiencies and fine-tune our approach before implementation.

Phase 3: Hardware Implementation

Project Overview

Our Smart Integrated Traffic System uses ultrasonic sensors and Arduino technology to optimize traffic flow, enhance pedestrian safety, and prioritize emergency vehicles. By detecting vehicles in real time, the system automatically adjusts traffic signals, ensuring smoother movement at intersections while also providing buzzer alerts for pedestrian crossings. With an emergency vehicle priority feature, it allows faster response times for ambulances and other critical services.
